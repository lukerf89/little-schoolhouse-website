You're Not Alone

Building community one family at a time

Homeschooling thrives in community. Connect with other Arkansas families who understand your journey, share your values, and celebrate your victories.

The Heart of Homeschooling

When we started homeschooling, we thought it would be just our family around the kitchen table. What we discovered was a vibrant community of families supporting each other, sharing resources, and creating lifelong friendships.

"The homeschool community in Arkansas has become our extended family. Our children have friends who share their values, and we parents have found our tribe." - Sarah M., Northwest Arkansas

Whether you're in Little Rock, the River Valley, Northwest Arkansas, or the smallest rural town, there's a homeschool community waiting to welcome you.

Starting Your Own Group

Can't find what you're looking for? Starting a homeschool group is easier than you think!

How to Begin

  1. Start Small: Begin with 2-3 families who share your vision
  2. Define Your Purpose: Academic co-op? Support group? Field trips?
  3. Choose a Meeting Place: Homes, churches, libraries, parks
  4. Set a Schedule: Weekly, bi-weekly, or monthly
  5. Spread the Word: Post in online groups, community boards
  6. Keep it Simple: Don't overcomplicate the first year

Ideas for Small Groups

  • Nature study group
  • Book club (moms or kids)
  • Art or music lessons
  • PE/sports activities
  • Science experiment days
  • History timeline projects
  • Foreign language practice

Meeting Spaces

  • Church fellowship halls
  • Community centers
  • Library meeting rooms
  • Parks and pavilions
  • Rotating homes
  • YMCA or rec centers
  • Coffee shops (for moms!)

Making Meaningful Connections

For Introverted Parents

  • Start with online connections first
  • Attend larger events where you can blend in
  • Find one family to connect with deeply
  • Volunteer for behind-the-scenes roles
  • Schedule regular breaks from group activities

For Extroverted Kids

  • Join multiple activities and groups
  • Encourage leadership roles in co-op
  • Host playdates and gatherings
  • Participate in team sports or drama
  • Connect with public school friends too
