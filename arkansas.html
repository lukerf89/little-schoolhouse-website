Arkansas LEARNS Act

Your guide to Education Freedom Accounts

The Arkansas LEARNS Act represents a significant shift in educational funding, providing families with more choices and resources for their children's education. Let's break down what this means for homeschool families.

What is the LEARNS Act?

Passed in 2023, the Arkansas LEARNS Act created Education Freedom Accounts (EFAs) that provide funding directly to families for educational expenses. This groundbreaking legislation gives parents the freedom to choose the best educational path for their children.

Key Points for Homeschool Families

  • Funding goes directly to families, not schools
  • Can be used for various educational expenses
  • Available to all Arkansas students, with phased rollout
  • Approximately $6,600 per student per year (90% of state foundation funding)
  • Families maintain homeschool freedoms while receiving support
"The LEARNS Act empowers parents to make the best educational decisions for their children, whether that's public school, private school, or homeschooling." - Arkansas Department of Education

Who Qualifies for Education Freedom Accounts?

The program is being phased in over three years to ensure smooth implementation:

2023-2024 School Year

Phase 1 Priority:

  • Students with disabilities
  • Foster children
  • Homeless students
  • Military families
  • Students previously in D or F rated schools

2024-2025 School Year

Phase 2 Expansion:

  • All Phase 1 categories
  • Families at or below 250% of federal poverty level
  • Approximately $51,500 for family of four

2025-2026 School Year

Universal Access:

  • All Arkansas students eligible
  • No income restrictions
  • First-come, first-served if demand exceeds funding

Important Note

Students must be Arkansas residents and between ages 5-21 (or not yet graduated from high school). Your child does not need to have previously attended public school to qualify.

How to Apply for an EFA

Step-by-Step Application Process

  1. Check Eligibility: Review the current phase requirements above
  2. Gather Documents:
    • Proof of Arkansas residency
    • Child's birth certificate
    • Income verification (if applying under income criteria)
    • Documentation for priority categories (if applicable)
  3. Create Account: Register on the Arkansas EFA portal (managed by ClassWallet)
  4. Submit Application: Complete online application during open enrollment
  5. Await Approval: Processing typically takes 2-4 weeks
  6. Account Activation: Once approved, funds are loaded quarterly

Application Timeline

  • March-April: Primary application window
  • May: Notifications sent
  • June-July: Account setup
  • August: Funds available for fall
  • Rolling basis: Applications accepted year-round as space allows

Required Commitments

  • Use funds only for approved educational expenses
  • Maintain expense documentation
  • Participate in state testing (grades 3-10)
  • Submit quarterly expense reports
  • Return unused funds at year end

What Can EFA Funds Cover?

Education Freedom Account funds can be used for a wide variety of educational expenses:

Curriculum & Materials

  • Textbooks and workbooks
  • Online curriculum subscriptions
  • Educational software
  • Library and reference materials
  • School supplies

Tutoring & Classes

  • Private tutoring
  • Online classes
  • Co-op fees
  • Music lessons
  • Art classes

Testing & Evaluation

  • Standardized test fees
  • AP exam fees
  • College entrance exams
  • Educational evaluations
  • Achievement tests

Therapy Services

  • Speech therapy
  • Occupational therapy
  • Behavioral therapy
  • Physical therapy
  • Educational therapy

Technology

  • Computers/tablets
  • Educational apps
  • Internet for schooling
  • Printers and supplies
  • Assistive technology

Other Expenses

  • Dual enrollment fees
  • Transportation (limited)
  • Field trip costs
  • PE equipment
  • Science lab materials

What's NOT Covered

  • General clothing (uniforms may be allowed)
  • Food and meals
  • Entertainment not directly educational
  • Parent payment for homeschooling
  • Religious materials (depends on vendor)

Managing Your EFA Account

ClassWallet Platform

Arkansas uses ClassWallet to manage EFA funds. This online platform allows you to:

  • View your account balance
  • Make purchases from approved vendors
  • Upload receipts for reimbursement
  • Track spending by category
  • Access your quarterly statements

Payment Methods

  • Direct Purchase: Buy from pre-approved vendors in the marketplace
  • Reimbursement: Pay out-of-pocket and submit receipts
  • ACH Transfer: For recurring expenses like tutoring

Best Practices

  • Keep all receipts organized by category
  • Review approved vendor list before purchasing
  • Submit reimbursements promptly
  • Plan your budget across all four quarters
  • Set aside funds for testing and evaluation
  • Check account regularly for updates

Quarterly Funding

Funds are distributed in four equal payments:

  • Quarter 1: August (Fall semester start)
  • Quarter 2: October
  • Quarter 3: January (Spring semester start)
  • Quarter 4: March

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I lose my homeschool freedom if I accept EFA funds?

You maintain your status as a homeschool family but must comply with EFA requirements, including state testing and expense reporting. You still choose your curriculum and teaching methods.

Can I use EFA funds for multiple children?

Each eligible child receives their own EFA account with separate funding. Funds cannot be transferred between siblings.

What happens to unused funds?

Unused funds must be returned at the end of the school year. They don't roll over, so plan your spending accordingly.

Can I use EFA funds for religious curriculum?

This depends on the vendor and how they're classified. Many faith-based curricula are available through approved vendors, but direct religious instruction may have restrictions.

How does testing work for EFA recipients?

Students in grades 3-10 must participate in state standardized testing. The state provides testing locations and schedules specifically for homeschool families.

Can I start mid-year?

Yes, applications are accepted on a rolling basis. If approved mid-year, you'll receive prorated funding for the remaining quarters.
